Historical Archives

Bernhard Riemann (1826-1866)

Riemann was a German mathematician who revolutionized calculus. Before him, the integral was seen as just a "reverse derivative." Riemann proved it was actually the limit of a sum, allowing us to calculate areas of incredibly complex shapes.

Fun Fact: The "S" Symbol

Did you know the integral symbol \(\int\) is actually a stylized "S"? It stands for "Summa" (Latin for sum), created by Leibniz to show that integration is just a very long addition process.

Calculus in Space

NASA uses Riemann Sums to calculate the trajectories of satellites. Since rocket fuel consumption isn't constant, they integrate the rate of fuel burn to find the total distance covered!
